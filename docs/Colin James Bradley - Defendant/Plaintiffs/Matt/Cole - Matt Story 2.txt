Colin James “Cole” Bradley entered my life by chance in Da Nang, Vietnam, wearing a hat claiming “infinite mileage” through zero-fuel propulsion. With my background in renewable energy and systems design, his claims seemed worth exploring. He positioned himself as the founder of Marine Warrior Superyachts, allegedly on the brink of launching revolutionary technology and multimillion-dollar yacht contracts. Within weeks, he recruited me to build his international marketing and operations architecture—offering $40,000 per month, equity, and full system-building authority. The materials he showed me—investor decks, presale contracts, spreadsheets, and manufacturing claims—appeared legitimate at first glance. As soon as I began working, the red flags multiplied. He never paid me. He failed to pay vendors. He ghosted deliverables. He made contradictory statements about investors, contracts, funding timelines, and manufacturing partners in Turkey, Thailand, the UAE, and Vietnam. Meanwhile, I was building his entire infrastructure—linking production sheets, drafting proposals, creating dataflows, handling communications, and covering costs myself for accountants, meals, transportation, rent, and even a moped. Every promise of reimbursement or payroll was pushed to “next week,” “after funding releases,” or “once Zurich clears the line.” His behavior grew more erratic. He belittled people in meetings, slammed his fist on tables, and emotionally manipulated anyone who challenged him. He cried frequently, claimed crises that never existed, and redirected blame onto others. Soon, victims surfaced everywhere: unpaid professionals, scammed builders, deceived women (including partners in China and Thailand), and former associates from Australia who warned us he had fled jurisdictions before. The pattern was global. The breaking point occurred during a final confrontation at a coffee shop in Da Nang. With my girlfriend present as a witness, I gave him the opportunity to provide even a single piece of proof backing his claims—Zurich financing, presale contracts, investor communications, manufacturing partners, or anything to validate his story. He produced nothing. Instead, he changed his story with every question, attempted emotional manipulation, and failed to offer any evidence. This was the moment I understood the entire operation was fabricated. I began documenting everything: screenshots, recordings, drive backups, contact lists of victims, and written testimonies. With the scale of harm becoming clear, I issued a formal Notice of Disassociation to all partners, suppliers, and professionals I had communicated with. This protected my reputation and ensured others would not be misled. Victims in multiple countries—Vietnam, Thailand, Australia, and the UAE—are now preparing to file reports, consult legal teams, and deliver documented evidence to authorities. Cole, consistent with his pattern, fled and has refused any accountability. The impact was significant—financially, professionally, and emotionally. I suffered out-of-pocket costs, lost time, damaged professional credibility through association with him, and severe stress from trying to stabilize a collapsing situation he created. Others suffered much more: unpaid livelihoods, emotional trauma, reputational harm, and damaged relationships. The case remains active as evidence continues to be gathered. This file ensures his pattern is documented publicly so that no one else is caught in the same deception he imposed on so many.
