# 📋 PLAINTIFF TESTIMONY QUESTIONNAIRE
## Court of Public Record - Voice Recording Guide

---

## 🎙️ HOW TO USE THIS DOCUMENT

**Instead of filling out our online form, you can record your story by speaking.**

1. Read through ALL the questions below
2. Use your phone's voice recorder app
3. Answer the questions in your own words - speak naturally
4. Send the recording to your admin contact
5. We'll organize your testimony and create your case

**Important:** You don't need to answer questions in order. Just tell your story and cover as much as you can. We'll follow up for anything missing.

---

## SECTION 1: DEFENDANT INFORMATION

**Tell us about the person or entity who harmed you:**

1. What is the defendant's full legal name? (First, middle, last)
2. Does this person use any aliases, nicknames, or alternate names?
3. Where does the defendant currently live? (City, state, country - even approximate is helpful)
4. How old is the defendant, or what is their date of birth?
5. What social media profiles does the defendant have? (Facebook, LinkedIn, Instagram, etc.)
6. Does the defendant own or operate any businesses? What are the business names?
7. Does the defendant have any websites or online domains?
8. Does the defendant have any known associates or accomplices?
9. What dates did this situation occur? (Example: "2018 to 2020" or "June 2019")
10. What type of case is this? (Fraud, breach of contract, defamation, harassment, assault, theft, employment dispute, real estate dispute, family law, or other)

---

## SECTION 2: YOUR CONNECTION

**Tell us about your relationship with the defendant:**

1. How did you know the defendant? (Employer, business partner, romantic partner, family member, friend, client, customer, landlord/tenant, professional associate, stranger, other)
2. How did you first meet this person? Tell the story.
3. When did your relationship with them begin? (Approximate date is fine)
4. When did the relationship end?
5. Who introduced you, or how did you connect?
6. What made you trust this person initially?
7. Looking back, were there any early warning signs you missed?

---

## SECTION 3: THE PROMISE

**Tell us what was promised to you:**

1. What promise or commitment did the defendant make to you? Be specific.
2. Was this promise made in writing? (Contract, email, text message)
3. Were specific amounts of money promised? How much?
4. Were specific dates or timelines promised?
5. What specific deliverables or outcomes were promised?
6. Were there any witnesses present when these promises were made?
7. What evidence do you have of these promises? (Emails, texts, contracts, recordings)
8. Did the defendant show you any proof they could actually fulfill these promises?

---

## SECTION 4: THE BETRAYAL

**Tell us when and how things went wrong:**

1. When did you first notice something was wrong? (Date if you remember)
2. What was the first red flag or warning sign?
3. Describe how the lies escalated. What was the pattern?
4. How did the defendant respond when you confronted them?
5. Did they use gaslighting or manipulation tactics? Describe what they did.
6. Did you receive any threats? What were they?
7. What was the final incident that ended your relationship?

---

## SECTION 5: FINANCIAL IMPACT

**Tell us about the money you lost:**

1. What is the total amount you lost? (Estimate if you don't know exactly)
2. How much did you pay directly to the defendant?
3. Did you lose wages or income because of this? How much?
4. Did you lose property or was property damaged? What's the value?
5. Did you have to pay legal fees? How much?
6. Did you have medical costs related to this incident? How much?
7. Was your credit score damaged? By how much (estimate)?
8. Were there other financial losses? Describe them.
9. What have you done to try to recover your money?
10. What's the current status of financial recovery?
11. Have you recovered any money so far? How much?

---

## SECTION 6: PERSONAL IMPACT

**Tell us how this affected you personally:**

### Emotional Impact
1. Did you experience emotional or psychological harm?
2. Describe how this affected you emotionally.
3. Did you need therapy or counseling?
4. Were you diagnosed with PTSD?
5. Were you diagnosed with depression?
6. Were you diagnosed with anxiety?
7. Did you experience suicidal thoughts?
8. Do you now have difficulty trusting people?

### Physical Impact
1. Did you experience any physical harm or injuries?
2. Describe any physical injuries.
3. What medical treatment did you need?
4. Are there ongoing health issues?

### Career Impact
1. Did this affect your career or employment?
2. Describe any damage to your professional reputation.
3. What career opportunities did you lose?

### Relationship Impact
1. Did this affect your relationships with family or friends?
2. Describe how your personal relationships broke down.
3. How was your family affected?
4. Were children affected by this situation?

### Ongoing Consequences
1. Are there ongoing consequences you're still dealing with?
2. Do you have safety concerns?
3. Are you experiencing financial instability?
4. Are there ongoing legal issues?

---

## SECTION 7: TIMELINE OF EVENTS

**Walk us through what happened, step by step:**

For each important event that happened, tell us:

1. What happened? (Be specific - describe the event)
2. When did it happen? (Date if you remember, or approximate)
3. What time of day did it happen?
4. Where did it happen? (Address or location description)
5. What city and country?
6. What type of event was this? (Meeting, payment, phone call, incident, discovery, other)
7. Who witnessed this event?
8. Do you have evidence of this event? (Photos, documents, recordings)
9. Why is this event important to your case?

**Tell the story chronologically if you can, or just tell it however you remember it. We'll organize it for you.**

---

## SECTION 8: EVIDENCE YOU HAVE

**Tell us what evidence you have:**

For each piece of evidence, tell us:

1. What type of evidence is it? (Email, text message, photo, video, document, contract, bank statement, recording, social media post, witness statement, police report, court document, medical record, other)
2. Give it a short title or name
3. Describe what it shows or proves
4. What date is this evidence from?
5. Which timeline event does this evidence relate to?

**Note:** You don't need to send the files yet. Just tell us what you have. We'll help you upload them later.

---

## SECTION 9: OTHER PEOPLE AFFECTED

**Tell us about others involved:**

### Other Victims
1. Do you know of other people this defendant harmed?
2. What are their names? (If they're comfortable sharing)
3. How do you know they were also victimized?
4. Do their stories show similar patterns?
5. Are they willing to come forward publicly?

### Witnesses
1. Who witnessed what happened to you?
2. What are their names and how can we contact them?
3. What is their relationship to you and the case?
4. What did they witness?
5. Do they have a written statement?

---

## SECTION 10: LEGAL ACTIONS TAKEN

**Tell us about any legal steps you've taken:**

For each legal action, tell us:

1. What type of action? (Police report, civil lawsuit, restraining order, criminal charges, regulatory complaint, bankruptcy claim, small claims court, other)
2. When did you file it? (Date)
3. Where did you file it? (Which court, agency, city)
4. What's the case number? (If you have it)
5. What's the current status? (Active, dismissed, judgment obtained, appeal, settled, other)
6. What was the outcome or current result?
7. If you got a judgment, how much was it for?
8. Can you actually collect on the judgment?
9. Do you have related documents?

---

## SECTION 11: YOUR STORY SUMMARY

**In your own words:**

1. Give us one sentence that summarizes your entire case.
2. Tell us the opening of your story - how did this all begin?
3. Tell us the main events - what happened in the middle?
4. Tell us the conclusion - where are things now and what impact did this have?
5. Now tell us the complete detailed story from beginning to end. Don't hold back - this is your chance to tell everything.

---

## SECTION 12: SAFETY & PRIVACY

**Tell us about your comfort level:**

1. Do you want to use your full name on the case, just your first name, or remain anonymous?
2. Do you have safety concerns about going public with this case?
3. What specific safety concerns do you have?
4. Are there minors (children under 18) involved who need to be protected?

## 🎯 RECORDING TIPS

### Before You Start:
- Find a quiet place
- Have any documents nearby for reference (dates, amounts, names)
- Take your time - you can pause and continue later
- It's okay to say "I don't know" or "I don't remember" for some questions

### While Recording:
- Speak clearly but naturally
- Don't worry about perfect grammar - just tell your story
- If you make a mistake, just correct yourself and keep going
- Focus on facts: dates, names, amounts, locations, what was said, what happened
- Quote exact words when you can remember them
- It's okay to express emotion - this is your story

### What NOT to Worry About:
- Speaking in order (we'll organize it)
- Perfect structure (we'll format it)
- Every single detail (we'll follow up for what's missing)
- Sounding professional (authenticity is more important)
