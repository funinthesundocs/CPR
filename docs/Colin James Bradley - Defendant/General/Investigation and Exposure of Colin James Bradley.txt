Investigation and Exposure of Colin James Bradley: Comprehensive Briefing

Executive Summary

This document synthesizes the findings and collective testimony of several individuals associated with Colin (Cole) James Bradley. Based on internal communications between victims and former business associates, Bradley is characterized as a serial con man operating across Australia, the United Arab Emirates (UAE), Thailand, and Vietnam.

The central findings indicate a consistent pattern of fraudulent behavior, including the solicitation of investments for non-existent or failing boat-building enterprises, non-payment of employees and contractors, and personal deceptions involving his family and financial status. Currently, Bradley is reported to be in Vietnam, largely destitute and seeking new victims to provide for his basic needs. In response, a collective of victims is developing a digital "Court of Public Record" to document his history, provide a warning to the public, and seek potential restitution through a shared platform.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------


Profile of the Subject: Colin James Bradley

Colin James Bradley (also referred to as Cole Bradley) is an Australian national currently located in Vietnam. The source context depicts a man whose professional life is built upon a "forked tongue" and a series of sophisticated, yet ultimately hollow, financial claims.

Core Behavioral Patterns

* Blame-Shifting: Bradley consistently attributes his financial failures to others. He has blamed his CFO (John Cooper) and external banking entities (3S Money, ADCB, Barclays) for his inability to access supposed millions in a family trust.
* Pathological Deception: He maintains a persona of a millionaire and super-yacht builder while simultaneously begging associates for small sums (80–100 AUD) for food and accommodation.
* Exploitation of Partners: Participants note a history of Bradley using women to fund his lifestyle and business ventures, frequently leaving them in significant debt.
* Cyclical Scamming: When one bridge is burned, he moves to a new jurisdiction (e.g., from Australia to UAE, then Thailand, then Vietnam) to restart the cycle.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------


Financial Deception and Fraudulent Tactics

Bradley utilizes several recurring "delaying tactics" to avoid paying debts or to solicit new funds.

The "Trust Fund" and Banking Narrative

Bradley claims to possess a family trust worth millions or access to a 450M–480M line of credit. However, he is unable to produce bank statements or evidence of these funds. He frequently cites technical banking errors, such as:

* Incorrect IBAN numbers.
* The "Barclays account" vs. "3S Money account" confusion.
* Solicitor errors in transferring funds.
* Accounts being "red-flagged" due to the actions of his employees.

Professional Fraud

* Falsified Sales: Bradley has claimed to have sold over 400 boats, yet his CFO reports no evidence of sales contracts, deposits, or trading figures.
* Unpaid Wages: One associate reported 2.5 years of unpaid wages and expenses. Bradley’s standard response to payment demands is to request a "breakdown of work" that generated income, effectively using the company's lack of revenue—which he caused—as a reason to withhold pay.
* Identity Exploitation: He has used the names of others to secure loans for companies under their names, leaving the individuals liable for the debt.

Solicitation of New Investment

As recently as early 2026, Bradley was observed seeking "buy-ins" for his company (e.g., $240,000 AUD) on social media platforms like Instagram and Threads, promising "free e-foils" with boat purchases despite having no manufacturing facility or display models.

Personal Deceptions and Alleged Misconduct

The source context reveals a history of personal misconduct that Bradley denies or manipulates to gain sympathy.

* Abandonment of Children: Participants provide evidence of at least three children (Lyla, Josh, and a third identified by child support) whom Bradley has allegedly denied or failed to support. He has claimed the children are not his to avoid child support, despite physical resemblances noted by others.
* Fabricated Pregnancy: Bradley claimed a Chinese woman named "Sissy" was pregnant with his only child to solicit money from John Cooper. Sissy later confirmed she was merely a former neighbor, was not interested in him, and the child in question belonged to her ex-husband.
* Sexual Misconduct: Testimony from a housekeeper in Vietnam alleges that Bradley exposed himself to her and begged for sexual favors.
* Theft: One former partner (Kelly) alleged that Bradley stole money from her wallet while visiting her apartment in Vietnam.

The "Court of Public Record" (CPR) Initiative

A collective of victims, led by Matt Campbell, has launched a digital counter-offensive to stop Bradley from victimizing others.

Project Goals

1. Exposure: The websites www.colinjamesbradley.com and www.courtofpublicrecord.com serve as a "digital prison." They are designed to rank highly in search results so that banks, potential partners, and women can find a "red flag" warning.
2. Testimony Hosting: The platform allows victims to upload timelines of events, evidence, and personal testimony.
3. Restitution Model: The project aims to generate ad revenue from the traffic generated by these high-profile cases, with 50% of proceeds intended to go back to the victims as restitution.
